# Free Groups

## Definition

The **free group** F(X) on a set X = {x₁, x₂, ...} is the group of all reduced words in X ∪ X⁻¹ (where X⁻¹ = {x₁⁻¹, x₂⁻¹, ...}) with concatenation followed by free reduction as the group operation.

A **reduced word** contains no subword of the form xxˉ¹ or xˉ¹x for any x ∈ X. Free reduction: repeatedly cancel adjacent inverse pairs until no more cancellations are possible. The empty word ε is the identity.

**Universal property:** For any group G and any function f: X → G, there is a unique group homomorphism F(X) → G extending f. F(X) is the "most general" group generated by X with no relations.

*Source: D&F §6.3, Proposition 6; Lyndon-Schupp §I.1.*

## Why it matters

Every group G with generating set X is a quotient F(X)/N for some normal subgroup N (the normal closure of the relations). Free groups are the universal starting point for group presentations: G = ⟨X | R⟩ means G = F(X)/⟪R⟫. Understanding G means understanding what gets killed by ⟪R⟫.

The word problem in F(X) is solvable in O(|w|) time (free reduction to unique normal form). This is the base case; adding relators makes the problem harder, and eventually undecidable.

## Key properties

- **Word problem:** decidable in linear time (reduce to unique reduced word; check if empty).
- **Hopfian:** every surjective endomorphism of F(X) (X finite) is an isomorphism (Hopf's theorem).
- **Residually finite:** every non-identity element maps to a non-identity element in some finite quotient.
- **Rank:** |X| is the **rank** of F(X). F(m) ≅ F(n) iff m = n (for infinite X: both are infinite and countable).

## Examples

- **F(∅):** the trivial group {e}.
- **F({x}) = F₁:** infinite cyclic group ℤ (free group of rank 1).
- **F({a,b}) = F₂:** the free group of rank 2 — contains free subgroups of every finite rank (and uncountably many distinct subgroups).
