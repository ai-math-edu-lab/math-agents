# On a subgroup of the Burnside group B₀(2,5)

> **Translation note**: source paper is in Russian (`language: ru`). Abstract below is translated to English [trans.] from the mathnet.ru record.

## Abstract

The author investigates a subgroup $G = \langle xy, yx \rangle$ generated within the universal 2-generated finite group of exponent 5. Key findings include: the group has order $5^{14}$, nilpotency class equals 6, and derived length equals 3. The author constructs both lower and upper central series and demonstrates that $G$ is the largest 2-generated group of exponent 5 and nilpotency class 6. [trans.]

## TL;DR

Computes the structure of $G = \langle xy, yx \rangle \le B_0(2,5)$: order $5^{14}$, nilpotency class 6, derived length 3. Proves $G$ is the largest 2-generated group of exponent 5 and nilpotency class 6.

## Problem

What is the structure of the subgroup $G = \langle xy, yx \rangle$ of $B_0(2,5)$ (where $x = a_1$, $y = a_2$ are the generators)? This is a natural subgroup generated by the two non-trivial products of the two generators.

## Approach

Explicit computation within $B_0(2,5)$ using the power commutator representation from [[havas-wall-wamsley-1974]]. Constructs the lower central series ($G > G' > G'' > \ldots > 1$, length = derived length 3) and upper central series ($1 < Z(G) < Z_2(G) < \ldots < G$, length = nilpotency class 6). Proves maximality of nilpotency class 6 among 2-generated exponent-5 groups.

## Key result

For $G = \langle xy, yx \rangle \le B_0(2,5)$:
- **Order**: $|G| = 5^{14}$
- **Nilpotency class**: 6
- **Derived length**: 3
- **Maximality**: $G$ is the largest 2-generated group of exponent 5 with nilpotency class 6 (among all such quotients of $B_0(2,5)$).

## Assumptions

- $B_0(2,5)$ finite with standard generators $x = a_1$, $y = a_2$.
- Power commutator representation from [[havas-wall-wamsley-1974]] correct.

## Limitations / scope

- Specific to the subgroup generated by $xy$ and $yx$; other subgroups of $B_0(2,5)$ have different structure.
- Does not address $B(2,5)$ directly.

## Replication evidence

The values $|G| = 5^{14}$, class 6, derived length 3 are verifiable using KBMAG, GAP, or any B₀(2,5) computation tool. No published replication known as of 2026-05-28, but the abstract is specific enough that any independent implementation can check.

## Why this paper matters

$|G| = 5^{14}$ is a concrete subgroup size benchmark. For the Mixer's B(2,5) pipeline:
1. **Target word check**: the commutator word $xy \cdot yx^{-1}$ (if different from $e$) in $B(2,5)$ must, if $B(2,5) \cong B_0(2,5)$, generate a subgroup of the same structure as described here.
2. **Class-6 bound**: knowing that the class-6 layer of B₀(2,5) has a 2-generated subgroup of order $5^{14}$ pins down part of the nilpotency tower, which guides ordering choices in KB experiments.
3. **Empirical calibration point**: $5^{14}$ elements, class 6, is small enough to enumerate and cross-check against the growth function data in [[kuznetsov-2016]].

## Open questions surfaced

- Does the analogous subgroup $\langle xy, yx \rangle$ in the free $B(2,5)$ (if finite) have the same order $5^{14}$ and nilpotency class 6? If yes, that's a constraint on $B(2,5)$.
- What is the structure of $\langle x^2y, yx^2 \rangle$ or other similar products?
