# Solving olympiad geometry without human demonstrations

> **AlphaGeometry research line**: This is AlphaGeometry 1 (AG1), the foundational paper. The successor [[2502.03544]] (AlphaGeometry2, 2025) extends AG1 to more problem types and achieves gold-medalist-level performance. Both papers should be read as a single line of work. AG1 was also part of the IMO 2024 system described in [[alphaproof-2024]].

## Abstract

AlphaGeometry is a theorem prover for Euclidean plane geometry. Rather than relying on human-demonstrated proofs, the system "synthesizes millions of theorems and proofs across different levels of complexity." This neuro-symbolic approach combines a neural language model with a symbolic deduction engine to navigate complex problem spaces. On 30 olympiad-level geometry problems, AlphaGeometry solved 25 — substantially outperforming prior methods that solved only 10. The system's performance approaches that of "an average International Mathematical Olympiad (IMO) gold medallist." Notably, it generates "human-readable proofs" and successfully addressed all geometry problems from IMO 2000 and 2015 under expert evaluation. An additional achievement: discovering "a generalized version of a translated IMO theorem in 2004," demonstrating capacity for mathematical insight beyond basic problem-solving.

## TL;DR

Neuro-symbolic system for Euclidean geometry olympiad problems: a language model trained on millions of synthetically generated geometry theorems guides a symbolic deduction engine through the proof search. Solved 25/30 olympiad problems, approaching gold-medalist level — the first AI to demonstrate this on geometry olympiad problems without human-written proof demonstrations.

## Problem

Olympiad geometry requires not just deduction but *constructive insight*: adding auxiliary constructions (new points, lines) that aren't in the problem statement but are key to the proof. Prior symbolic geometry provers fail because they don't know which auxiliaries to add; prior LLMs fail because they can't guarantee deductive validity. Can a neuro-symbolic system combine both?

## Approach

**Dual-component architecture:**
1. **Symbolic deduction engine**: applies geometry deduction rules exhaustively from given elements. Cannot make forward progress when it needs an auxiliary construction.
2. **Language model**: trained on **millions of synthetically generated** geometry theorems (generated by the symbolic engine itself, with random auxiliary constructions). When the symbolic engine stalls, the LM proposes a new auxiliary construction.

**No human demonstrations needed**: the training data is entirely synthetic — the symbolic engine generates random theorems and proofs; the LM learns from these.

**Iterative search**: alternate between LM-proposed auxiliaries and symbolic deduction until either the proof is found or resources are exhausted.

## Key result

- **30 olympiad problems (IMO competition set)**: AlphaGeometry solved **25**. Previous best: 10.
- **IMO 2000 + IMO 2015 geometry**: solved all problems under expert evaluation.
- **Silver-medalist level** on the test set (25/30 ≈ 83%).
- **Novel result**: discovered a generalized version of an IMO 2004 theorem — evidence of beyond-memorization mathematical insight.
- **Human-readable proofs**: the system produces proofs that can be followed by human mathematicians.

## Assumptions

- Euclidean plane geometry is the domain (angle chasing, circle theorems, etc.); other geometry types (3D, projective) are out of scope.
- Symbolic engine covers a sufficient rule set for olympiad geometry.
- Synthetic data (millions of generated theorems) adequately samples the relevant distribution of olympiad-style problems.

## Limitations / scope

- **Euclidean plane geometry only** (AG1 scope). AG2 ([[2502.03544]]) extends to movements of objects and linear equations of angles/ratios.
- The approach relies on a specialized geometry formal language (not Lean or Isabelle) — less general than proof-assistant-based systems.
- Does not handle non-geometric olympiad problems (algebra, number theory, combinatorics).

## Replication evidence

Google DeepMind released code at https://github.com/google-deepmind/alphageometry. The methodology has been extended in [[2502.03544]] by the same group, confirming the core approach is sound.

## Why this paper matters

AlphaGeometry demonstrates that **massive synthetic data generation + neuro-symbolic integration** can reach near-human performance on hard mathematical problems without human demonstrations. This is a paradigm shift from prior work that required annotated human proofs:

1. **Synthetic data at scale**: generating millions of theorems internally (rather than relying on existing library) is now a template for ATP training. [[2504.21801]] (DeepSeek-Prover-V2) uses a recursive version of this: generate subgoal problems internally.
2. **Neuro-symbolic integration**: the alternation between neural (LM for auxiliaries) and symbolic (deduction engine for correctness) is distinct from purely neural systems. This matters for reliability — every deduction step is formally verified.
3. **Human-readable proofs**: demonstrates that formal correctness and human readability are not in tension.

**Cross-vault connection**: the auxiliary-construction insight in AlphaGeometry is structurally analogous to what a Mixer Agent does when injecting rules from another ordering into a KB computation — both systems add new objects (auxiliary constructions / injected rules) that the local algorithm couldn't generate on its own. The multi-source insight pattern is the same.
