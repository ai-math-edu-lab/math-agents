# Gold-medalist Performance in Solving Olympiad Geometry with AlphaGeometry2

> **AlphaGeometry research line**: This is AlphaGeometry 2 (AG2), the direct successor to [[trinh-2024-alphageometry]] (AG1, Nature 2024). AG2 was also the geometry component of the [[alphaproof-2024]] IMO 2024 silver-medal system. Read all three notes together for the full line of work.

## Abstract

"We present AlphaGeometry2 (AG2), a significantly improved version of AlphaGeometry introduced in (Trinh et al., 2024), which has now surpassed an average gold medalist in solving Olympiad geometry problems. To achieve this, we first extend the original AlphaGeometry language to tackle problems involving movements of objects, and problems containing linear equations of angles, ratios, and distances. This, together with support for non-constructive problems, has markedly improved the coverage rate of the AlphaGeometry language on International Math Olympiads (IMO) 2000-2024 geometry problems from 66% to 88%. The search process of AG2 has also been greatly improved through the use of Gemini architecture for better language modeling, and a novel knowledge-sharing mechanism that enables effective communication between search trees. Together with further enhancements to the symbolic engine and synthetic data generation, we have significantly boosted the overall solving rate of AG to 84% on all geometry problems over the last 25 years, compared to 54% previously. AG2 was also part of the system that achieved the silver-medal standard at IMO 2024."

## TL;DR

AG2 extends AG1's Euclidean geometry scope (movements, linear equations, non-constructive problems), improves the LM (Gemini), adds knowledge-sharing between search trees, and scales synthetic data. Result: 66%→88% language coverage on IMO 2000-2024 geometry; 54%→84% solving rate; surpasses average gold medalist. AG2 solved Problem 4 at IMO 2024 in 19 seconds.

## Problem

AG1 ([[trinh-2024-alphageometry]]) is limited to constructive Euclidean plane geometry (angle chasing, circle properties, etc.) with 66% language coverage on real IMO geometry problems. The remaining 34% involve problem types AG1 can't express: movements of geometric objects, linear equations of angles/ratios/distances, non-constructive existence proofs. Can AG2 close this gap?

## Approach

**Four key extensions over AG1:**

1. **Extended geometry language**: adds support for (a) problems involving movements of objects (e.g., "as P moves along the circle"), (b) linear equations of angles, ratios, and distances (enabling problems with metric constraints), (c) non-constructive problems (existence proofs, not just constructions).

2. **Stronger language model**: Gemini-based architecture (vs. AG1's custom transformer), trained from scratch on an "order of magnitude more" synthetic data.

3. **Knowledge-sharing mechanism**: a novel technique enabling communication between search trees in parallel search instances — effectively sharing discovered auxiliary lemmas across independent proof attempts.

4. **Improved symbolic engine + synthetic data generation**: incremental improvements to the deduction engine and data generation pipeline.

## Key result

- **Language coverage** on IMO 2000-2024 geometry: **66% → 88%** (AG1 → AG2). The remaining 12% are primarily non-Euclidean or projective geometry.
- **Overall solving rate** on 25 years of IMO geometry: **54% → 84%** (AG1 → AG2).
- **Gold-medalist level**: AG2 surpasses the average gold medalist on geometry problems.
- **IMO 2024**: solved Problem 4 (geometry) in **19 seconds** as part of the [[alphaproof-2024]] system.
- Open source code: https://github.com/google-deepmind/alphageometry2.

## Assumptions

- The extended AG language (with movements, linear equations) covers the new 22% of IMO geometry problems.
- Gemini architecture + more synthetic data generalizes better than AG1's custom transformer.
- Knowledge-sharing between search trees provides efficiency gains without correctness risks.

## Limitations / scope

- 12% of IMO geometry still outside AG2's language (projective, 3D, non-Euclidean).
- Geometry only — AG2 does not address algebra, number theory, or combinatorics.
- The 84% rate is on the AG language subset; actual coverage of ALL IMO geometry (including the 12% outside language) is lower.

## Replication evidence

Code open-sourced. AG2's IMO 2024 Problem 4 result (19-second solve, independently verified by IMO jury) is the primary external validation.

## Why this paper matters

AG2 crosses two thresholds:
1. **Gold-medalist level on geometry**: surpassing the average gold medalist is the competitive milestone that establishes AI as better than the top ~10% of human IMO competitors on geometry.
2. **Language coverage 88%**: by covering 88% of IMO geometry, AG2 makes geometry a nearly solved domain for AI. The remaining 12% defines the next research frontier.

The key technical contribution — knowledge-sharing between search trees — is a general technique for multi-tree parallel search that could apply beyond geometry (e.g., parallel KB completion in the Mixer).

**Cross-vault connection**: the knowledge-sharing mechanism between search trees in AG2 is conceptually related to the Mixer's rule injection between parallel KB agents (Agent A injects rules discovered by Agent B). The parallel is not exact (different formal systems) but the insight is the same: parallel search improves when independent search paths share discoveries.
