# Cayley graphs of Burnside groups

> **Concept hub.** Multiple Kuznetsov papers study the Cayley graph geometry of finite Burnside groups and their subgroups. This hub distinguishes the Cayley-graph-as-object from the Cayley-table-closure algorithm ([[Concepts/cayley-table-closure-algorithm]]) — different things despite the similar name.

## Definition

The **Cayley graph** $\Gamma(G, S)$ of a group $G$ with symmetric generating set $S$ is the directed graph where:
- **Vertices**: elements of $G$
- **Edges**: $(g, gs)$ for each $g \in G$, $s \in S$

For Burnside groups in Kuznetsov's work, the generating sets are $S = \{a_1, a_2\}$ or $S = A_4 = \{a_1, a_1^{-1}, a_2, a_2^{-1}\}$, and the groups are the quotients $B_k$ of $B_0(2,5)$ (order $5^k$), their centralizers, or the smaller exponent-3/7 Burnside groups.

Key graph-theoretic quantities studied:
- **Diameter** $D_S(G) = \max\{d_S(e,g) : g \in G\}$ — the longest shortest path from identity to any element.
- **Average diameter** $\bar{D}_S(G)$ — mean distance from identity across all group elements.
- **Growth function** $f_S(k)$ — see [[Concepts/growth-functions-burnside]].

**Distinction from Cayley-table-closure algorithm ([[Concepts/cayley-table-closure-algorithm]])**: The Cayley *table* is the multiplication table $T[g,h] = gh$ for all $g, h \in G$ — an algebraic object. The Cayley *graph* is the distance-based graph structure — a combinatorial/geometric object. Kuznetsov's Algorithm I builds a Cayley table; these papers study Cayley graph geometry. Both use the same name "Cayley" but are different constructs.

## Why it matters

Cayley graph diameter data for Burnside groups:

1. **Bounds the search horizon for the Mixer**: if $D_{A_4}(B_0(2,5)) \approx 69$, the bidirectional search must meet within ~35 steps from each end. Rules that are further from identity than the diameter bound cannot be generated by simple BFS.
2. **Subgroup structure via geometry**: centralizer Cayley graphs ([[kuznetsov-kuznetsova-2017]]: $D = 33$ for order-$5^{17}$ centralizer; [[kuznetsov-safonov-2018]]: geometry of order-$5^{16}$ centralizer) reveal how the group's symmetry is encoded geometrically.
3. **Cross-exponent validation**: exponent-3 Burnside Cayley graphs beat hypercubes ([[kuznetsov-2015-cayley-exp3]]) — a topological property that may generalize to exponent 5.
4. **Network topology applications**: Burnside group Cayley graphs are proposed as multiprocessor interconnect topologies (diameter minimization, vertex-transitivity). This is the practical motivation in [[kuznetsov-2015-cayley-exp3]].

## Open questions

- **Exact diameter of $B_0(2,5)$** ($k = 34$): currently ~105 (2-gen) and ~69 (4-gen) estimated from $k = 18, 19$. Direct computation would settle this.
- **Do exponent-5 Burnside Cayley graphs beat hypercubes?** Established for exponent-3 ([[kuznetsov-2015-cayley-exp3]]); not yet shown for exponent-5.
- **Girth and connectivity**: Kuznetsov's papers focus on diameter; girth (shortest cycle) and connectivity have not been studied in this vault.
- **Is the diameter hypothesis** $D_{A_4}(eN_k) = D_{A_4}(B_k)$ ([[kuznetsov-2019]]) provable in general?
