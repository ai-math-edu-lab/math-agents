## Tag-format gap + frontmatter-discipline lesson 2026-05-22

### Tag-format issue surfaced by Human's audit

Human audited tags across the vault. Found that Developer's Components/ notes (11+ files) use a non-canonical YAML tag form:

  tags:
    - "#agent/dev"
    - "#user/maumayma"
    ...

Obsidian does NOT parse this form. Quoted strings with `#` prefix in YAML lists are opaque to Obsidian's tag indexer and Bases tag-filter queries. The canonical format used everywhere else (`_progress.md`, paper notes, Concepts):

  tags: [agent/dev, user/maumayma, domain/<area>, project/<...>, status/draft, <content-tag>]

No `#`. No quotes. Inline array. The `#` is for body-text inline tagging ONLY.

Functionally, Developer's Components/ tags are invisible to Obsidian — they "pass" a structural `^tags:` grep but fail the semantic test of whether the consumer (Obsidian's parser) recognizes them.

### Lead self-noted gap: probe must match consumer's parser

My earlier K3 spot-check on Developer's notes verified `^tags:` was present (structural check). It did NOT verify the tag values are in Obsidian's parseable format (semantic check). Another flavor of probe-before-assume: the verification must match what the downstream consumer actually parses, not just what's syntactically present.

**Binding rule on Lead going forward**: when K3-checking tag arrays, verify (a) `tags:` is present, (b) it contains the 5-axis minimum, AND (c) values are written without `#` prefix and without quotes (so Obsidian parses them). Or grep for the canonical inline-array form: `grep -E "^tags: \\[" <file>`.
